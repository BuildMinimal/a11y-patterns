Why Colour Contrast Matters More Than You Think

By Jordan Lee · 12 April 2026 · 5 min read

Colour contrast is often treated as a checkbox — run the tool, hit 4.5:1, move on. But the real impact of poor contrast extends far beyond users with low vision. Bright sunlight, cheap monitors, tired eyes after a long day: these affect everyone.

WCAG 2.2 requires a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text. These thresholds are not arbitrary — they are derived from decades of vision science research and represent the point at which most people with moderate low vision can reliably read text.
